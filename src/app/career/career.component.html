Motivation


Motivation is an internal emotional driver of behavior. Many times management feel like they can motivate people, however this is incorrect. Managers can create an environment that stimulates core emotional drivers of employees in order that they can motivate themselves. (Dahlke, 2015) Knowing about motivation will allow me to better discuss what motivates me. First of all let me tell you what does not motivate me, money. What does motivate me? Systems and making systems work. Details motivate me. Having say in the design an implementation of projects. Autonomy motivates me. Time with my family motivates me.

Integrity


I am a rule follower. More than just a rule follower I believe in the uttermost honesty in all business dealings. For example, when asked for funnel forecasts I will only give an accurate funnel based on documented commitments and conversations with clients. I believe it is immoral to “fluff” numbers to make myself look better. If there is something I have done that could have made a negative experience for a coworker or the client I have a moral imperative to claim responsibility for the circumstance. Also, I will always be completely honest with a customer or co-workers, even if it costs me money.

I have been a sales manager, product manager and corporate trainer. However, the majority of my time has been in sales. I have multiple years in both retail and business sales. In all of my sales history, my success has come via my in depth knowledge on a wide variety of technical solutions. My track record comes from having an ability to digest large amounts of data, understand the material and be able to communicate it in a way easy for people and businesses to understand. Along with my wireless experience I have also been an EMT in a Urology Clinic, an entrepreneur, a theology minister, couples counselor in a church and a musician.
